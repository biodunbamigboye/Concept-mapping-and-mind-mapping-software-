Computer Based Mapping Strategy || About

Computer-based concept map is a computerized graphical representation of number of concepts. The Concepts are usually enclosed in circles or boxes, and linking words or phrases linking two concepts to indicate the relationship between the concepts. Computer-based mind map on the other hand is a computerized graphic organizer of ideas and a visible drawing that indicates what happens in the process of storing information in brain. Mind map involves a unique combination of imagery, color and visual spatial arrangement.

How it works

Tree expansion and collapsing process

To expand the Mapped tree, click on the plus(+) button beneath the node.

To  collapse a section of the map click on the minus(-) button beneath the parent node on the map

We have two different view points.

  1. Full Screen View(Suppurts Zooming)
  2. Responsive Screen View(Autoscales to screen size)

Zooming of map

Use the "plus" and "minus" button to zoom in or zoom out the map respectively.

(Not supported in lesson one and two of Concept mapping)

Use the navigation button on the keyboard to navigate the map after zooming the map

There are also two scroll bars at both vertical and horizontal ends of the map, this can be used to navigate through the map for easier view

Magnifying Glass (Supported in concept map only)

This is used to magnify the text on each node of the map for clearer view.

To activate this click magnifier icon at the right top handside of the screen.

To deactivate this function. Take the magnifier outside the mapping area and click on the magnifier icon to deactivate.

Drag and drop function

You can drag any of the nodes and dragging it to any other part of the map.

to drag click any of the nodes and drag to the desired location

Computer Based Mapping Strategy || Objectives

After going through this package, you should be able to:

  1. Define rates of chemical reactions.
  2. To determine the gradient of the curve at given time or instant.
  3. Explain the collision theory.
  4. Explain nature of reactants as factor that affects rate of a chemical reaction.
  5. Explain effect of change in pressure as factor that affects rate of a chemical reaction.
  6. Explain effect of change in concentration as factor that affects rate of a chemical reaction
  7. Explain effect of light as factor that affects rate of chemical a reaction.
  8. Explain effect of change in temperature as factor that affects rate of a chemical reaction
  9. Explain effect of surface area as factor that affects rate of a chemical reaction.
  10. Define catalyst.
  11. States types of catalyst.
  12. Draw energy profiles for catalyzed reaction.
  13. Explain exothermic reaction.
  14. Explain endothermic reaction
  15. Explain ΔH notation
  16. State characteristics of a catalyst
  17. State examples of catalyst of some common reactions
